# Heat Transfer in a Cooling Fin using ANSYS

This project presents a thermal analysis of a cooling fin subjected to both convection and radiation using ANSYS Workbench.

## 📌 Overview
Cooling fins are widely used in engineering systems to enhance heat dissipation from heated surfaces. This project models and simulates the thermal behavior of a rectangular cooling fin to analyze temperature distribution, heat flux, and thermal gradients under steady-state conditions.

The simulation was carried out using ANSYS to study the effectiveness of fin-based heat dissipation in thermal management systems such as electronics cooling and automotive applications.

---

## 🛠️ Methodology
The cooling fin model was created and analyzed using the following steps:

- Geometry creation in ANSYS DesignModeler
- Mesh generation for computational analysis
- Assignment of material properties
- Application of boundary conditions (convection & radiation)
- Execution of steady-state thermal simulation
- Post-processing to obtain temperature and heat flux distribution

## 🌡️ Temperature Distribution
The temperature is highest at the base of the fin (heat source) and decreases gradually along the length towards the tip, indicating efficient heat dissipation.

![Temperature Distribution](images/temperature_distribution.png)

---

## 🔥 Heat Flux Distribution
Heat flux is observed to be maximum near the base region due to a higher temperature gradient and decreases towards the fin tip.

![Heat Flux](images/heat_flux.png)

---

## 📈 Key Observations
- Efficient heat dissipation along fin length
- Maximum heat flux near the base region
- Significant thermal gradients near heat source
- Simulation results align with theoretical heat transfer models
